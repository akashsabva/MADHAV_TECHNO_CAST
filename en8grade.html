EN8 Carbon Steel – Chemical Composition & Equivalent Grades

EN8 Carbon Steel is a popular medium carbon steel known for its excellent machinability and strength. This article provides in-depth insights into its composition, properties, and various equivalents across global standards.

What is EN8 Carbon Steel?

EN8 is a medium strength steel grade with good tensile strength and is suitable for applications requiring better properties than mild steel but not as demanding as alloy steels. It is typically supplied in untreated or normalized condition.

Applications

  • Automotive components
  • Shafts, spindles, and axles
  • Machine tools and gears
  • Crankshafts and connecting rods
  • Forgings and general engineering parts

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages:

  • Good machinability and workability
  • Improved strength over mild steels
  • Can be heat treated for better performance

Limitations:

  • Limited corrosion resistance
  • Requires caution during welding
  • Not suitable for very high-strength applications

Conclusion

EN8 Carbon Steel remains a staple material in the engineering world thanks to its combination of strength, machinability, and adaptability. For applications where durability is critical but alloy steel is not required, EN8 serves as a cost-effective and reliable choice.
